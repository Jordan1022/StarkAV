# Stark AV Source Of Truth

Last updated: June 2, 2026

## Company Identity

**Company:** Stark AV  
**Founder:** Isaiah Stark  
**Primary audience:** Churches and ministry teams that need thoughtful audio, video, production, and training support.  
**Secondary audience:** Corporate conference rooms and government meeting halls that need practical AV design or consulting.

Stark AV provides AV design, consulting, install support, and team training with a support-first posture. The business should feel like a trusted partner for churches rather than a vendor trying to sell equipment. The work should be framed around stewardship, clarity, confidence, and serving the church body well.

## Mission And Positioning

Stark AV exists to help churches use audio, video, and production systems in ways that serve their people and support the broader mission of spreading the gospel.

Core positioning:

- Be a support for churches, not just a seller of equipment.
- Be for the church and its volunteers.
- Recommend what will benefit the church body, not what is most expensive.
- Help teams feel confident, consistent, and well-rounded during services.
- Design systems that serve the room, the people, and the ministry vision.

Tone:

- Warm, practical, and steady.
- Technically credible without sounding cold or overly corporate.
- Ministry-aware without becoming overly slogan-driven.
- Clear and direct, with language that churches can understand.

## Services

### AV Design And Consulting

Stark AV designs and advises on audio, video, and production systems for churches. Engagements may include assessment, system design, recommendations, documentation, budgeting guidance, and technical consultation.

### Install Support

Stark AV can be as involved in the install process as the church wants. That may include helping select an installer, coordinating with an integration partner, reviewing installation decisions, or handling more of the process on behalf of the church.

### Church Production Team Training

Training for production volunteers should help teams understand their systems, serve with consistency, and feel more confident in their roles during services.

### Worship Team Training

Training can support worship teams as they work with production systems, stage sound, monitoring, service flow, and healthy collaboration with production volunteers.

### Worship Leadership Team Training

Training can support worship leaders and ministry leaders with the practical decisions that shape Sunday services, team communication, production expectations, and long-term system stewardship.

### Light Corporate And Government Consulting

Stark AV can lightly support corporate and government AV needs, especially conference rooms and meeting halls. This should remain secondary to the church-focused positioning.

## Brand Direction

Use the supplied Stark AV logo as style guidance. The logo uses a bold red-orange mark on a neutral gray field, with a simple modern wordmark and an AV symbol.

Recommended visual direction:

- Clean, restrained, confident, and practical.
- Red-orange accent color inspired by the logo.
- Neutral backgrounds that feel warm enough for churches but not beige or rustic.
- Minimal decoration; let clarity, typography, and real project imagery carry the site.
- Avoid fake dashboards, fake metrics, aggressive sales language, and overly tech-startup visuals.

Starter brand values:

- Supportive
- Technically capable
- Ministry-minded
- Clear
- Reliable
- Stewardship-focused

## Featured Work Proof Point

Isaiah Stark's system design work was featured in FOH Online's article, [Houston's Villa de Matel Chapel Upgrades with dBTechnologies System](https://fohonline.com/featured/houstons-villa-de-matel-chapel-upgrades-with-dbtechnologies-system/), published February 3, 2026.

Relevant proof points from the feature:

- Isaiah Stark is identified as the system designer for AV Design Pros on the Villa de Matel Chapel project.
- The chapel was completed in 1927 and required a visually discreet, architecturally sensitive sound reinforcement approach.
- The project focused on spoken-word intelligibility, preserving the room's natural character, minimizing feedback risk, and keeping technology visually restrained.
- The system design used EASE Focus 3 and Smaart.
- The project also included hard-wired audio integration for livestream, a new mixer for EQ and control, and camera system upgrades.

This proof point should be used as evidence of Isaiah's ability to design systems that solve real acoustic and ministry problems without making the technology the center of attention.
